# Protecting Sensitive Data & Information

#### What Is Sensitive Data?

**Sensitive data** is information that must be protected to prevent harm, misuse, or unauthorized access.

Examples of sensitive data include:

* Passwords and authentication codes
* Personal Identifiable Information (PII)
* Student, employee, or customer records
* Financial or payment information
* Confidential business or academic data

Protecting sensitive data is a shared responsibility.

***

#### Why Protecting Sensitive Data Matters

When sensitive data is exposed, it can lead to:

* Identity theft
* Financial loss
* Academic or professional harm
* Privacy violations
* Legal or regulatory consequences
* Loss of trust

Many security incidents cause lasting impact long after the initial exposure.

***

#### Common Ways Sensitive Data Is Exposed

Sensitive data is often compromised through:

* Phishing or social engineering attacks
* Weak or reused passwords
* Lost or stolen devices
* Unsecured file sharing
* Improper disposal of documents
* Oversharing via email or messaging tools

Data breaches frequently begin with simple mistakes.

***

#### Data Classification and Awareness

Not all data should be treated the same way.

Sensitive data typically requires:

* Limited access
* Secure storage
* Careful transmission
* Proper disposal

Understanding what data is sensitive helps users protect it appropriately.

***

#### Safe Handling of Sensitive Information

Best practices for handling sensitive data include:

* Sharing only with authorized individuals
* Using secure platforms and approved tools
* Encrypting files when required
* Avoiding sending sensitive data via email or text
* Double‑checking recipients before sending information

Pause and verify before sharing sensitive information.

***

#### Storing Sensitive Data Securely

Sensitive information should be stored in secure locations, such as:

* Approved cloud storage
* Encrypted systems or devices
* Managed databases or platforms

Avoid storing sensitive data:

* On personal devices without approval
* In unsecured documents
* In easily accessible locations

Secure storage reduces exposure risk.

***

#### Disposing of Sensitive Data

Proper disposal is just as important as secure storage.

Best practices include:

* Shredding physical documents
* Securely deleting digital files
* Following organizational retention policies

Improper disposal can expose data long after it is no longer needed.

***

#### Data Protection in Public and Shared Environments

Extra caution is needed in public or shared spaces:

* Avoid viewing sensitive data on public screens
* Lock devices when unattended
* Limit conversations involving sensitive topics
* Be mindful of shared printers and workstations

Environmental awareness supports data protection.

***

#### What To Do If Sensitive Data Is Exposed

If you believe sensitive data has been exposed:

1. Stop further sharing immediately
2. Secure affected accounts or systems
3. Report the incident according to organizational procedures
4. Preserve relevant details if possible

Prompt reporting helps reduce impact.

***

#### Key Takeaways

* Sensitive data requires extra protection
* Not all information should be shared freely
* Email and messaging are common exposure points
* Secure storage and disposal are critical
* Awareness reduces the risk of data breaches
