# Securing Your Devices (Laptops, Phones, Tablets)

#### Why Device Security Matters

Laptops, phones, and tablets store and access large amounts of sensitive information, including:

* Email and messaging accounts
* Passwords and authentication tokens
* Personal, academic, or work data
* Access to cloud services and systems

If a device is lost, stolen, or compromised, attackers may gain access to multiple accounts and services.

***

#### Common Risks to Unsecured Devices

Devices that are not properly secured may be vulnerable to:

* Unauthorized access if lost or stolen
* Malware infections
* Credential theft
* Data exposure or loss
* Account compromise across multiple services

Many security incidents begin with a single unsecured device.

***

#### Locking Your Device

One of the simplest and most effective protections is using a **device lock**.

Best practices include:

* Using a strong PIN or password
* Enabling biometric authentication when available
* Setting devices to auto‑lock after short periods of inactivity

A locked device significantly limits access if it falls into the wrong hands.

***

#### Keeping Devices Updated

Just like desktops and servers, personal devices require regular updates.

Ensure that:

* Operating systems are kept up to date
* Applications are updated regularly
* Security updates are not skipped or disabled

Updates help protect devices from known vulnerabilities that attackers actively exploit.

***

#### Installing Apps and Software Safely

Only install apps and software from **trusted sources**, such as:

* Official app stores
* Trusted vendors
* Organizationally approved platforms

Be cautious of:

* Apps requesting excessive permissions
* Free or unofficial software downloads
* Applications advertised through pop‑ups or ads

Untrusted apps can introduce malware or collect sensitive data.

***

#### Protecting Devices in Public and Shared Spaces

When using devices in public or shared environments:

* Do not leave devices unattended
* Be mindful of who can see your screen
* Avoid sharing devices when signed in
* Log out of accounts when finished

Physical access to a device can be just as dangerous as remote access.

***

#### Lost or Stolen Devices

If a device is lost or stolen:

1. Notify IT or support staff immediately (if applicable)
2. Change passwords for important accounts
3. Disable or sign out of sessions remotely if possible
4. Monitor accounts for suspicious activity

Quick reporting reduces the risk of further compromise.

***

#### Personal vs. Organizational Devices

Devices used for work or school may have additional security requirements, such as:

* Approved security software
* Restricted configurations
* Monitoring or management tools

Follow organizational policies when using devices for institutional access.

***

#### Key Takeaways

* Devices are gateways to multiple accounts and systems
* Locking and updating devices provides strong baseline protection
* Untrusted apps increase security risk
* Physical security is as important as digital security
* Prompt reporting protects accounts and data
