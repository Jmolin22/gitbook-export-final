# Phishing Awareness & Email Safety

**What Is Phishing?**

Phishing is a type of cyber attack where an attacker attempts to trick someone into revealing sensitive information or taking an unsafe action by pretending to be a trusted source.

Common goals include:

* Stealing login credentials
* Installing malware
* Gaining access to accounts or systems

&#x20;

**Why Phishing Still Works**

Phishing succeeds because it:

* Exploits trust and urgency
* Looks increasingly legitimate
* Targets human behavior, not just technology

Even experienced users can be fooled under the right conditions.

&#x20;

**Common Signs of a Phishing Email**

Watch for emails that include:

* Urgent or threatening language
* Unexpected attachments or links
* Requests for passwords, codes, or payment
* Slight misspellings in sender names or domains
* Messages that “feel off,” even if branding looks correct

&#x20;

**Best Practices to Reduce Risk**

* Do not click links or open attachments you weren’t expecting
* Verify requests through a second channel
* Hover over links before clicking
* Use multi‑factor authentication (MFA)
* Report suspicious emails when possible

&#x20;

**Real‑World Scenario**

You receive an email claiming to be from your IT department stating your account will be locked unless you verify your credentials immediately.

Safer response:

* Do not click the link
* Check the sender address carefully
* Contact IT through known, trusted contact information
* Report the message if reporting is available

&#x20;

**Key Takeaway**

Phishing attacks are designed to create urgency and bypass careful thinking.

When in doubt: pause, verify, and report.
