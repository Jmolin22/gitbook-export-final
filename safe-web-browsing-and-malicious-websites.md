# Safe Web Browsing & Malicious Websites

#### What Is Safe Web Browsing?

**Safe web browsing** means using the internet in a way that reduces the risk of malware infections, account compromise, and data theft.

Attackers often use **malicious or compromised websites** to deliver scams, steal credentials, or install harmful software. These sites may appear legitimate and trustworthy at first glance.

***

#### Why Web Browsing Can Be Risky

Simply visiting the wrong website or clicking the wrong link can expose users to:

* Fake login pages designed to steal credentials
* Malicious downloads disguised as legitimate files
* Pop‑ups that claim a device is infected
* Redirects to scam or impersonation sites

Many web‑based attacks rely on **user trust and inattention**, not technical vulnerabilities.

***

#### Look‑Alike and Impersonation Websites

Attackers often create websites that closely resemble legitimate ones by:

* Using misspelled domain names\
  (example: replacing letters or adding extra words)
* Imitating branding, logos, and layouts
* Copying login pages from real services

These sites are commonly used for credential theft and phishing campaigns.

***

#### Understanding HTTPS and Padlock Icons

Seeing a padlock or **HTTPS** in the browser address bar means the connection is encrypted — **not** that the website is safe or legitimate.

Important points:

* HTTPS protects data in transit
* HTTPS does _not_ verify the intent or trustworthiness of a website
* Malicious websites can also use HTTPS

Always verify the domain name, not just the padlock icon.

***

#### Pop‑Ups, Fake Alerts, and Scare Tactics

Malicious websites often use pop‑ups or alerts that claim:

* Your device is infected
* Your account is compromised
* Immediate action is required

These messages are designed to create urgency and fear.

Common goals include:

* Convincing users to download malware
* Redirecting users to scam services
* Harvesting payment or personal information

***

#### Unsafe Downloads and Free Software

Downloading software, files, or browser extensions from untrusted sources increases risk.

Be cautious of:

* “Free” software from unknown websites
* Files attached to pop‑ups or ads
* Browser extensions requesting excessive permissions

Only download software from official or trusted sources.

***

#### Best Practices for Safe Web Browsing

To reduce risk when browsing the web:

* Verify website addresses carefully before entering information
* Avoid clicking links from unknown or unexpected sources
* Be skeptical of urgent or alarming messages
* Close suspicious pop‑ups instead of interacting with them
* Keep browsers and operating systems up to date

Safe browsing is largely about **slowing down and verifying before acting**.

***

#### What To Do If You Visit a Suspicious Website

If you believe you’ve visited a malicious or suspicious site:

1. Close the browser tab immediately
2. Do not download or run any files
3. Do not enter any credentials
4. Report the site according to your organization’s process
5. Monitor accounts for unusual activity

Early reporting can help prevent wider impact.

***

#### Key Takeaways

* Not all threats arrive by email
* Malicious websites can look legitimate
* HTTPS alone does not guarantee safety
* Pop‑ups and scare tactics are common attack methods
* Careful verification reduces risk
